## Part I: Getting Started in Texas (Chapters 1–4)

### Chapter 1: Why Texas Is Different (8–10 pages)
- Texas's 5 distinct growing regions (map included)
  - North Texas / DFW (Zone 7b–8a)
  - Central Texas / Hill Country (Zone 8a–8b)
  - Gulf Coast / Houston (Zone 9a)
  - South Texas / Rio Grande Valley (Zone 9b–10a)
  - West Texas / El Paso (Zone 7b–8a, arid)
- The 2026 USDA Hardiness Zone Map — what changed and why it matters
- Two growing seasons: spring and fall (the Texas advantage)
- The Texas summer: managing the "dead zone" (July–August)
- Average first/last frost dates by region

### Chapter 2: Understanding Texas Soil (12–15 pages)
- Soil types across Texas: black clay (Blackland Prairie), sandy loam (East TX), caliche (Central/West TX), sandy (Gulf Coast)
- Soil testing: how, where, and what the numbers mean
- Amending clay soil without losing your mind
- Building raised beds: the Texas gardener's best friend
- Composting in Texas heat
- Mulching strategies for water conservation

### Chapter 3: Water-Smart Gardening (10–12 pages)
- Water restrictions and conservation mindset
- Drip irrigation setup (step-by-step)
- Soaker hoses vs. sprinklers vs. hand watering
- Rainwater harvesting (legal in TX, encouraged)
- Mulching to reduce water needs by 50%
- Ollas and other low-tech watering solutions
- Watering schedules by season and region

### Chapter 4: Planning Your Texas Garden (10–12 pages)
- Choosing your garden style: in-ground, raised bed, container, or hybrid
- Sun mapping your yard (8+ hours for most vegetables)
- Garden sizing for beginners vs. experienced growers
- Crop rotation basics (3-year plan)
- Succession planting for continuous harvests
- Companion planting quick-reference chart
- Tool essentials — what you actually need

## Part II: Month-by-Month Growing Guide (Chapters 5–16)

### Chapter 5: January — Planning & Prep
- Order seeds (recommended Texas seed companies)
- Start indoor seeds: tomatoes, peppers, eggplant
- Garden bed prep and soil amendment
- Cover crop management
- Cold frame and row cover techniques
- **Regional notes:** South TX can direct-sow cool-season crops; North TX is dormant

### Chapter 6: February — Early Spring Action
- Transplant cool-season crops: broccoli, cabbage, lettuce, kale
- Direct sow: peas, radishes, carrots, beets, spinach
- Potato planting (Valentine's Day tradition)
- Prepare tomato and pepper transplants
- Frost protection strategies
- **Regional notes:** Gulf Coast & South TX are 2–4 weeks ahead of North TX

### Chapter 7: March — Spring Planting Surge
- THE big planting month for most of Texas
- Transplant tomatoes and peppers (after last frost)
- Direct sow: beans, squash, cucumbers, corn, melons
- Herb planting: basil, cilantro, dill, parsley
- Pest watch: aphids, cutworms, flea beetles
- Trellis and support installation

### Chapter 8: April — Peak Spring Garden
- Succession plantings of beans, squash, cucumbers
- Sweet potato slips go in
- Okra and southern peas direct sow
- First harvests: lettuce, radishes, peas, greens
- Pest management ramps up
- Mulch everything — heat is coming

### Chapter 9: May — Harvesting & Heat Prep
- Major harvest month: tomatoes, peppers, squash, beans
- Remove cool-season crops that are bolting
- Plant heat-lovers: okra, sweet potatoes, black-eyed peas
- Shade cloth installation for sensitive crops
- Fire ant management in the garden

### Chapter 10: June — Summer Strategy
- Harvest tomatoes before extreme heat sets in
- Maintain okra, peppers, sweet potatoes
- Summer cover crops in empty beds
- Water management intensifies
- Planning the fall garden (yes, already)
- Seed ordering for fall crops

### Chapter 11: July — The Dead Zone (Surviving Summer)
- What still grows: okra, sweet potatoes, peppers (some), yard-long beans
- Summer garden maintenance: water, mulch, weed
- Start fall transplants indoors (tomatoes, peppers, broccoli, cauliflower)
- Solarizing empty beds to kill weeds and pathogens
- Soil amendments for fall beds

### Chapter 12: August — Fall Garden Launch
- THE most important month for Texas fall gardening
- Transplant fall tomatoes and peppers
- Direct sow: squash, cucumbers, beans (round 2!)
- Start cool-season transplants: broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage
- Dealing with spider mites and heat stress

### Chapter 13: September — Fall Planting Push
- Direct sow cool-season crops: lettuce, spinach, kale, turnips, beets, carrots
- Transplant broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, Brussels sprouts
- Herb planting: cilantro (finally!), dill, parsley
- Fall pest management
- Rain returns — adjust watering

### Chapter 14: October — Cool Season Peak
- Continue planting greens, root vegetables
- Garlic planting (Columbus Day tradition)
- Onion planning (Texas supersweets!)
- First fall harvests
- Cover crops for empty beds
- Frost date awareness for North TX

### Chapter 15: November — Late Fall & First Frost
- Plant onion transplants (1015s and other Texas varieties)
- Protect tender crops from first freeze
- Harvest sweet potatoes before hard frost
- Fall/winter greens under row covers
- Garden cleanup and bed prep for winter
- **Regional notes:** South TX still planting; North TX protecting

### Chapter 16: December — Winter Gardening & Reflection
- Winter greens harvest: kale, collards, spinach, lettuce (under cover)
- Garden journaling and record-keeping
- Seed catalog review and ordering
- Tool maintenance and storage
- Cold frame gardening for year-round greens
- Planning next year's garden layout
