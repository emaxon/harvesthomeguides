# Florida Vegetable Gardening — Chapter Outline

## Part I: Getting Started in Florida
1. **Chapter 1: Why Florida Is Different** — Year-round growing, 3 zones (North/Central/South), USDA zones 8b–11a, two main seasons (fall & spring), why summer is the off-season, frost dates
2. **Chapter 2: Understanding Florida Soil** — Sandy soil reality, nematodes in the ground, pH challenges, raised beds, soil amendments, composting in humidity
3. **Chapter 3: Water Management** — Rain patterns, irrigation in sand, hurricane season water strategy, rainwater harvesting, drip systems, dealing with flooding
4. **Chapter 4: Planning Your Florida Garden** — Garden styles, sun mapping, sizing, crop rotation, succession planting, companion planting intro, essential tools

## Part II: Month-by-Month Growing Guide
5. **Chapter 5: January** — Cool-season harvests, frost protection North FL, planting potatoes, starting spring seeds indoors
6. **Chapter 6: February** — Spring planting begins, tomato/pepper transplants, last frost North FL, direct sow warm crops South FL
7. **Chapter 7: March** — Peak spring planting, warm-season crops go in statewide, herb planting, pest watch begins
8. **Chapter 8: April** — Spring harvests begin, last cool-season crops come out, mulch heavily, irrigation ramp-up
9. **Chapter 9: May** — Major harvests, heat intensifies, summer strategy, pest pressure peaks
10. **Chapter 10: June** — Summer survival mode, tropical crops only, cover crops, hurricane prep begins
11. **Chapter 11: July** — The dog days, maintain what's growing, plan fall garden, start fall seeds indoors
12. **Chapter 12: August** — Fall planting begins (South FL), seed starting for fall (Central/North), soil prep
13. **Chapter 13: September** — Fall planting surge, cool-season crops go in, hurricane season peak
14. **Chapter 14: October** — Peak fall planting, garlic, greens galore, pest management transitions
15. **Chapter 15: November** — Cool-season garden peaks, frost prep North FL, strawberry planting
16. **Chapter 16: December** — Winter harvests, garden reflection, seed ordering, cold protection
